FAQ's


Q: When is ODYSSEY?

A: The festival dates work around the Woman's Day weekend (9 Aug). This year the festival will be on 7, 8, 9, August 2015. Camping Gates open 5 August at noon Festival area gates open 7 August at 9am Camping site closes Monday 10 August Entrance and exit to festival gates are closed between 1am and 5am daily for security purposes

Q: Where will ODYSSEY take place?

A: ODYSSEY takes place in Northam in Limpopo. It's about a 3 hour drive from Johannesburg.

Q: Where do I get tickets?

A: Tickets are only sold via the website, do not buy resale tickets or overpriced tickets from 3rd party resellers such as Viagogo. Tickets will be available at the gates.

Q: Are there day passes?

A: Yes. We do have day passes, it can be specified on the tickets page.

Q: I haven't received my ticket; help?

A: Please contact Plankton.mobi, they have an online contact and help system on their website, otherwise please mail info@odyssey.co.za.

Q: Can I bring my own booze to the festival?

A: Own alcohol is allowed however, in the main entertainment area no glass will be allowed.

Q: How do I book a tent hotel?

A: Tent hotel tickets exclude festival tickets, they open a short while after general tickets go on sale and can also only be purchased via the site.

Q: Will there be an ATM at ODYSSEY?

A: All the festival stalls and bars only operate on the Cashless system, so no cash will be needed, the card top-up can be done with cash or bank cards; so you should be fine.

Q: What should I bring?

A: Wetwipes would be the best place to start. Apart from that it is clean clothes, a tent, Sunscreen, water and as many real friends as you can gather. Keep an eye out for festival survival guides on social media.

Q: What if I lose any of my valuables at ODYSSEY?

A: There will be a lost-and-found where you can report, drop off or collect lost items, and we will be posting pictures of all the lost items on our Facebook page after the event to reunite you and your valuables.

Q: Are hubblies allowed?

A: Only in the camping area. Glass becomes a problem in the entertainment area.

Q: How does it work this year with the public holiday?

A: The public holiday falls on Sunday the 9th of August, so Monday will be a public holiday. Camps have to be cleared on Monday 10 Aug.
